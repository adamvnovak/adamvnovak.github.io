Economic Cost of Distraction

Many of the biggest tech companies, such as Meta, Snap, and YouTube, make their money through the attention economy. Google and Facebook are both at their core advertising companies, and they make more money the more time you spend using their products. Even non- attention-dependent companies like video game makers still aim to make the most entertaining video games, which keeps us playing longer as a byproduct. Video games are generating loads of revenue. Countries like South Korea have pivoted to making video games a core part of their economy, as something to be cultivated. In the short term, our economy appears to be doing the best when kids are playing Valorant instead of studying and when adults are watching Reels instead of working. Yet this clearly doesn't add up.
